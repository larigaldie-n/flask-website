Cognition, Behaviour, Statistics and Programming

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Aarhus University, where I am investigating the mechanisms underlying (in)attendance to information that contradicts prior beliefs. I received my PhD in Psychology from Durham University, with a focus on cognitive modeling using computational methods and Bayesian statistics. I am an unapologetic science nerd in general, as I also studied Computer science, worked as a programmer for several years, and still spend quite some time learning statistics and discussing epistemology (you know, just for fun).

My research interests are broad and include, but are not limited to:

  • Beliefs and misinformation in the internet era
  • Epistemology, philosophy of science, meta-science... (however you want to call it!)
  • Computational modeling of human cognition

I believe these three topics to be of prime importance for improving modern society and science, which is why I am a vocal supporter of some of their implications. Especially teaching critical thinking in the general population and fighting misinformation, promoting open science and good statistical practices, and finally encouraging more mathematical and programming skills in behavioural & social sciences.
